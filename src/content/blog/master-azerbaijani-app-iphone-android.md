## Master Azerbaijani is now live

Master Azerbaijani is now available on both the **[Apple App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/master-azerbaijani/id6760097533)** and **[Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.masterazerbaijani.app)**.

This is a big milestone, but let me say it clearly: this is the first public version, not the finished version. The app is live, usable, and built to help learners start with structure. At the same time, audio, lessons, and future modules will continue to expand.

That is the honest version.


## Why I built Master Azerbaijani

I built Master Azerbaijani because I needed this app myself.

As a native Azerbaijani speaker living abroad, I always wanted to teach my husband and kids Azerbaijani. But between work, parenting, and daily life, it never became as consistent as I wanted it to be.

And I know I am not the only one.

Many Azerbaijani families abroad want their children, spouses, relatives, or close friends to learn Azerbaijani, but they do not always know where to start. Some learners know a few words but cannot build sentences. Some understand Azerbaijani but feel nervous speaking. Some are complete beginners who want a clear path instead of random phrases from the internet.

Master Azerbaijani was created for those learners: people who need a clear, structured path into the language.


## Who the app is for

Master Azerbaijani is built for:

- Heritage learners who want to reconnect with Azerbaijani  
- Families abroad who want to pass the language to the next generation  
- Spouses, partners, and in-laws of Azerbaijani speakers  
- Beginners who want a clear and practical introduction to the language  
- Anyone who wants to learn real Azerbaijani with cultural context  

The app is especially designed for people who need structure.

If you have ever tried to learn Azerbaijani by saving random Instagram posts, watching short videos, or asking family members to teach you “a few words,” you probably already know the problem.

Random exposure helps.

But random exposure does not always create real progress.

You need order.  
You need repetition.  
You need pronunciation support.  
You need to know what comes first, what comes next, and why it matters.

That is what the app is designed to provide.


## What you can do inside the app

The first version of Master Azerbaijani focuses on strong learning foundations.

Inside the app, learners can start working through structured lessons that cover beginner skills, including:

- Azerbaijani alphabet foundations  
- Pronunciation guidance  
- Practical vocabulary  
- Grammar patterns  
- Cultural context  
- Useful everyday phrases  
- Lesson-based practice  

The goal is not to overwhelm learners with too much at once. The goal is to help learners move through a clear path where each lesson builds on the previous one.

That matters because language learning gets frustrating fast when everything feels disconnected.

Master Azerbaijani is built to avoid that chaos.


## What is still expanding

Because this is the first public version, the app will continue to grow.

Native Azerbaijani audio is being expanded across lessons. Additional modules and lesson content will continue to be added over time. I am also listening closely to early users because real feedback matters more than building in a vacuum.

This is one of the advantages of launching now.

Instead of waiting until every future module is complete, I wanted learners to be able to start using the app while the course continues to expand. That means early users get to help shape the direction of the learning experience.

If something is confusing, I want to know.

If something is helpful, I want to know that too.

If a lesson makes you think, “I wish this were explained a little differently,” that feedback matters.


## Why this launch matters

Azerbaijani is not always easy to find in mainstream language-learning apps.

There are plenty of tools for Spanish, French, German, Italian, and other widely taught languages. But Azerbaijani learners often have to piece things together from YouTube videos, family explanations, scattered websites, or old textbook-style resources.

That is not good enough.

Azerbaijani deserves modern, structured, thoughtful learning tools too.

This app is my contribution to that.

It is built by a native speaker, shaped by diaspora experience, and designed for real learners who want more than memorized words.
