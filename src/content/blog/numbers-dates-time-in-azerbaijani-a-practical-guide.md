Numbers come up in every conversation.

Prices, phone numbers, addresses, times, dates, ages. You can get by without a lot of vocabulary, but without numbers you are stuck the moment someone asks how much something costs or when you need to be somewhere.

Azerbaijani numbers follow a logical system. Once you know the base numbers, everything else builds from them predictably.


## Cardinal numbers: the base

**0** sıfır  
**1** bir  
**2** iki  
**3** üç  
**4** dörd  
**5** beş  
**6** altı  
**7** yeddi  
**8** səkkiz  
**9** doqquz  
**10** on  

The tens follow the same logic. Learn these and you can build any number up to 99.

**20** iyirmi  
**30** otuz  
**40** qırx  
**50** əlli  
**60** altmış  
**70** yetmiş  
**80** səksən  
**90** doxsan  

To build numbers in between, you simply combine: tens first, then units.

> **11** on bir  
> **25** iyirmi beş  
> **43** qırx üç  
> **78** yetmiş səkkiz  

No connector word needed. The numbers sit next to each other.

For hundreds and thousands:

**100** yüz  
**200** iki yüz  
**500** beş yüz  
**1000** min  
**2000** iki min  

> **365** üç yüz altmış beş  
> **1990** min doqquz yüz doxsan  


## Ordinal numbers: first, second, third

Ordinal numbers are formed by adding a suffix to the cardinal number. The suffix follows vowel harmony.

**-ıncı / -inci / -uncu / -üncü**

> **birinci** - first  
> **ikinci** - second  
> **üçüncü** - third  
> **dördüncü** - fourth  
> **beşinci** - fifth  
> **onuncu** - tenth  

Ordinals are used for floors, rankings, positions, and dates.

> **Birinci mərtəbə.** - *First floor.*  
> **İkinci qapı.** - *Second door.*  


## Telling the time

To ask the time:

> **Saat neçədir?** - *What time is it?*

To answer, use **saat** (hour/clock) followed by the number.

> **Saat birdir.** - *It is one o'clock. (1:00)*  
> **Saat üçdür.** - *It is three o'clock. (3:00)*  
> **Saat on ikidi.** - *It is twelve o'clock. (12:00)*  

For minutes past the hour, use **işləyir** (is working toward):

> **Saat ikiyə on dəqiqə işləyir.** - *Ten minutes past one. (1:10)*  
> **Saat beşə iyirmi işləyir.** - *Twenty past four. (4:20)*  

For minutes to the hour, use **qalıb** (is remaining):

> **Saat dördə on dəqiqə qalıb.** - *Ten to four. (3:50)*  
> **Saat yeddiyə beş dəqiqə qalıb.** - *Five to seven. (6:55)*  

Half past:

> **Saat üçün yarısıdır.** - *Half past two. (2:30)*  

For morning and afternoon:

> **səhər** - morning  
> **gündüz** - afternoon  
> **axşam** - evening  
> **gecə** - night  

> **Səhər saat səkkizdə.** - *Eight in the morning. (8:00 AM)*  
> **Gündüz saat dörddə.** - *Four in the afternoon. (4:00 PM)*  


## Days of the week

> **Bazar ertəsi** - Monday  
> **Çərşənbə axşamı** - Tuesday  
> **Çərşənbə** - Wednesday  
> **Cümə axşamı** - Thursday  
> **Cümə** - Friday  
> **Şənbə** - Saturday  
> **Bazar** - Sunday  

One thing worth knowing: **Bazar** means Sunday, not market (though the same word is used for market in some contexts). 


## Months of the year

> **Yanvar** - January  
> **Fevral** - February  
> **Mart** - March  
> **Aprel** - April  
> **May** - May  
> **İyun** - June  
> **İyul** - July  
> **Avqust** - August  
> **Sentyabr** - September  
> **Oktyabr** - October  
> **Noyabr** - November  
> **Dekabr** - December  

Azerbaijani month names are adapted from Russian/international usage and will feel familiar.


## Saying dates

Dates in Azerbaijani follow the order: day, month, year.

> **15 Yanvar** - *January 15*  
> **3 Mart 2026** - *March 3, 2026*  

To say "on" a date, use the locative suffix **-da / -də** on the day number, or the dative on the month:

> **15-də** - *on the 15th*  
> **Yanvarda** - *in January*  
> **2026-cı ildə** - *in the year 2026*  

For years, Azerbaijani uses the ordinal suffix on the year followed by **il** (year):

> **2026-cı il** - *the year 2026*  
> **min doqquz yüz doxsanıncı illər** OR **1990-cı illər** - *the 1990s*  


## Useful number phrases

**Neçə?** - *How many?*  
**Neçəyədir?** - *How much does it cost?*  
**Neçəncisiniz?** - *What number are you?* (in a queue)  
**Neçə yaşınız var?** - *How old are you?* (formal)  
**Neçə yaşın var?** - *How old are you?* (informal)  

> **Mənə iki dənə lazımdır.** - *I need two of them.*  
> **Üç gün sonra.** - *In three days.*  
> **İki həftə əvvəl.** - *Two weeks ago.*

## Numbers are the foundation of real conversation

You will use numbers every single day.

Prices, times, dates, quantities, ages, phone numbers, floor numbers, addresses. Getting comfortable with them early means one less thing to think about when a real conversation is already demanding enough.

Learn the base ten first. Everything else follows from there.
