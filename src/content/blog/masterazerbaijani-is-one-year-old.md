On November 1, 2025, I published the first blog post on this site.

It was about learning Azerbaijani without boring textbooks. I had no idea if anyone would read it.

A year later, I am writing this.


## Why I started

Most Azerbaijani learning resources are outdated, hard to find, or built for a very specific kind of learner. I wanted to build something different. Something that treats the language with the seriousness it deserves and the learner with the respect they deserve.

That is still the whole point.


## What the first year looked like

I set out to post a blog every ten days.

I did not miss one.

Thirty-seven posts published in the first year, covering grammar, culture, history, learning strategy, vocabulary, and everything in between. Some took a few hours. Some took much longer. Every single one was written with a specific learner in mind: someone who wants to actually understand the language, not just collect phrases.

Every day for a year, I posted a Word of the Day across [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/masterazerbaijani/), [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=61583629125594), [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@MasterAzerbaijani), and [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@masterazerbaijani). Each post was more than a single word: phrases, sentences, context, pronunciation. 365 days. No gaps.

And then there is the app.

The Master Azerbaijani app launched in March 2026 with five modules, the first one free on the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/master-azerbaijani/id6760097533) and then [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.masterazerbaijani.app). Five more followed in September. Five more are coming in March 2027. It is built to do what a blog cannot: give you structured, progressive practice that builds from foundations to real conversation. Grammar in context. Pronunciation. Everyday phrases. Cultural notes woven in throughout.

Building all of this in one year, alongside everything else life involves, was not small. I am proud of it.


## What I have learned in a year of doing this

Azerbaijani learners are more motivated than anyone gives them credit for.

The people who find this site are not casual. They are heritage speakers who feel the language slipping away and want it back. Diaspora parents who want their children to have something they almost lost themselves. People who fell in love with someone Azerbaijani and want to meet them in their language. Adults who simply decided it was time.

These are not people who need to be convinced that Azerbaijani matters. They already know. They just need somewhere to go.

I also learned that building something useful and building something visible are two different problems. The language learning space is crowded with content for Spanish, French, Japanese. Azerbaijani is quiet by comparison. Which means the people who need this often do not know it exists yet.

That is still the biggest challenge.


## What comes next

The blog keeps going. The app keeps growing.

The social channels will evolve. A year of daily posting taught me a lot about what resonates and what this community actually needs. Year two will reflect that.

What will not change is the mission. More people should be able to learn Azerbaijani well. That is still the whole point.

If you know someone who has been looking for something like this, sharing it is the most useful thing you can do.


## To everyone who has been here

Whether you found this site six months ago or last week, whether you have been reading every post or just started today: thank you for being here.

Azerbaijani is a beautiful language with a complicated history and a community of people who care about it deeply. I built this for them. For you.

Year two starts now.
